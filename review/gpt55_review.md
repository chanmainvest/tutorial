# GPT-5.5 Course Review

Status: in progress.

This review is being built file by file from the English markdown files under `course/`. Per-file critiques are stored in `review/gpt55/`.

## Working Standards

- Preserve depth: do not water down professional material, but improve scaffolding, pacing, examples, and emotional payoff.
- Keep the SOUL lens visible: teach orthodox professional finance first, then clearly mark Horace's retail-investor view where it differs.
- Make every lesson more actionable: define what the student can decide, calculate, avoid, or monitor after reading.
- Make YouTube scripts more watchable: stronger hooks, fewer lecture monologues, more tension, stakes, examples, visual beats, and audience-retention loops.
- Upgrade visuals: every difficult concept should have either a chart, decision tree, worked example, or interactive demo that changes a student's intuition.

## Overall Findings

Early signal: the course has a strong curriculum skeleton, but the front-door positioning is still too generic. It should more clearly sell the distinctive promise: professional toolkit plus retail-investor judgment, with Horace's SOUL.md philosophy as the differentiator.

Week 1 shows the course at its best: memorable thesis, strong charts, and a real Horace voice. The editorial challenge is to keep that edge while adding enough orthodox framing that skeptical students do not mistake the lesson for a political rant.

Week 2 is practical and high-value, especially on fees, advisor conflicts, and product traps. Its biggest gap is tax-jurisdiction practicality for non-US readers buying US-listed ETFs.

Week 3 has one of the best practical frameworks so far: risk capacity versus risk tolerance. This should become a recurring course-wide sizing rule.

Week 4 is a strong model for the course's desired structure: teach the orthodox baseline, identify the regime that made it work, then explain Horace's modern adaptation.

Week 5 is technically strong, especially on duration as the arithmetic behind 2022. Its main risk is fixed-income terminology precision.

Week 6 has a strong SOUL-aligned store-of-value frame. Its main improvement area is avoiding overclaiming: real rates matter greatly for gold, but CPI, central-bank buying, geopolitics, and reserve trust also matter.
