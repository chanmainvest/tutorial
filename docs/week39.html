Week 39: Futures — Micro E-mini, /MES, /MNQ, /MCL, Contango, and Section 1256

1. Why This Is Important

Futures are the oldest derivatives in the world. Cotton merchants in 1860s New Orleans, wheat farmers in 1880s Chicago, and Saudi crude desks in 1990s London all priced their goods on the same fundamental contract — I owe you 1,000 barrels (or bushels, or index points) on a fixed date at a fixed price, settled to cash daily. For 130 years that machine was sealed off from retail. The minimum contract size on the S&P 500 e-mini was $250,000 of notional with a $12,000 margin requirement; on crude oil it was 1,000 barrels at a $5,000 margin. You needed a five-figure subaccount to even try one position. Then in 2019 the CME launched the Micro E-mini (MES, 1/10th the size of ES) and the Micro WTI (MCL, 1/10th the size of CL), and what had been an institutional product became a retail one. As of April 2026 a single MES contract has roughly $25,000 of notional exposure and about $2,000 of initial margin. That is the size where it makes sense for a $50-100k brokerage account to consider it.

There are four reasons a Level-3 student needs to learn this lesson now.

  • Futures are the cleanest leverage in the toolkit. A long /MES is roughly 12-13x leverage on the cash you post as margin, with no margin interest, no overnight financing line, no maintenance call against the rest of your portfolio (other than the position itself), and no ETF expense ratio. Compare to SSO at 2x with 0.9% drag plus volatility decay (week 37), or to a LEAPS at 4x with extrinsic decay. Futures are the highest-leverage, lowest-cost way to express a directional view on the major indices.
  • Section 1256 tax treatment. This is one of the two SOUL #15 anchors in the entire course (the other is the long-LEAPS). Broad-based index futures and a small set of index options are taxed under §1256 of the IRC: 60% long-term capital gain, 40% short-term, regardless of holding period. A day-trade in /MES is taxed better than a day-trade in SPY, period. For high-bracket investors that is a 7-12 percentage point structural edge per dollar of gain.
  • Contango and backwardation explain the USO story you met in week 6. Crude oil ETFs, natural-gas ETFs, and VIX ETFs all roll a futures position monthly. When the curve is in contango — back month higher than front — the roll is a permanent drag on return. USO lost roughly 80% of its value in real terms 2009-2020 while spot WTI was essentially flat, almost entirely because of contango roll yield. You cannot understand commodity ETFs without understanding the futures curve, and the only way to fix the problem (when you actually want commodity exposure) is to take it through the futures themselves rather than through the wrapper.
  • The barbell needs a portable-exposure lever (SOUL #14). When the L1 sleeve is held in deep-ITM LEAPS and the L3 sleeve is generating cash from credit spreads (week 30), you sometimes want to flex aggregate beta up or down by 10-20 percentage points overnight. /MES is the right tool: post $2k of margin, control $25k of S&P exposure, taxed at 60/40, expires in three months. The position is sized in single-digit contracts, lasts a quarter, and rolls cleanly. SPY round-trips cost taxes; /MES round-trips do not, structurally.

    • This lesson is the operational manual for that fourth use case — and the cautionary tale for the first three regimes (over-leverage, contango drag, and the small-but-real path-dependence risk of daily mark-to-market).

    2. What You Need to Know

    2.1 What a Futures Contract Actually Is

    A futures contract is a standardised, exchange-traded agreement to exchange a fixed quantity of an underlying asset at a fixed price on a fixed future date. Unlike an option, both parties are obligated. Unlike a forward, the contract is standardised and cleared centrally, so counterparty risk runs to the exchange (CME), not to the trader on the other side.

    Three concrete examples used throughout this lesson:

    • /ES (E-mini S&P 500): $50 per index point. With the S&P 500 at 5,200 in April 2026, one /ES contract has notional $50 × 5,200 = $260,000. Initial margin: about $14,000 (5.4%). Tick size: 0.25 index points = $12.50.
    • /MES (Micro E-mini S&P 500): $5 per index point. Notional $26,000. Initial margin: about $2,000 (7.7%). Tick: 0.25 points = $1.25. This is the contract a $50-100k account uses.
    • /MNQ (Micro E-mini Nasdaq-100): $2 per index point. With the Nasdaq-100 at 19,000, notional $38,000. Initial margin: about $3,300. Tick: 0.25 points = $0.50.
    • /MCL (Micro WTI Crude Oil): 100 barrels per contract. With WTI at $72/bbl, notional $7,200. Initial margin: about $750. Tick: $0.01 = $1.00.
    • /MGC (Micro Gold): 10 troy ounces per contract. With gold at $2,400/oz, notional $24,000. Initial margin: about $1,500. Tick: $0.10 = $1.00.

    Equity-index futures are cash-settled at expiration — there is no physical delivery of 500 stocks. Crude and gold are physically deliverable; you must close or roll before the last trading day or you will be assigned a barge of barrels (in practice, every retail broker auto-liquidates 2-3 days before expiration).

    2.2 Notional vs Initial Margin — Why Leverage Is 12-15x by Default

    In stocks, when you "buy on margin," your broker lends you cash; the security itself is collateral, and you pay interest on the loan. Futures margin is not a loan. It is a performance bond — a refundable deposit the exchange holds to ensure you can settle the day's loss. The CME sets initial margin (what you must post to open) and maintenance margin (what you must keep above to avoid a call). Brokers can charge slightly more.

    [VISUAL: image/week39_micro_compare.png]

    The chart above asks one question: what is the cheapest way to control $50,000 of S&P 500 economic exposure?

    • SPY shares: $50,000. 1.0x leverage. Direct cost.
    • One MES contract: notional $26,000 × ~2 contracts ≈ $50,000 exposure for ~$4,000 of margin. ~12.5x leverage.
    • One ES contract: $260,000 of notional, oversized for a $50k target — uses ~$14,000 of margin to control 5x the exposure. The wrong tool below $200k of intended position size; you cannot dial in finely.
    • One LEAPS at 0.92Δ on SPY (week 37): ~$10,000 of premium for $52,000 of exposure. ~5x leverage.

    The futures path uses 1/12th the capital of the share path. The freed cash sits in T-bills earning 4.3% (April 2026), exactly as in the LEAPS chapter — but with a 60/40 tax envelope on the futures gain rather than long-term capital gains on the shares.

    The cost: mark-to-market. Every trading day at 4 p.m. CT, the exchange computes your gain or loss and sweeps it from your account in cash, even if you have not closed. If MES drops 100 points overnight (a 2% move) on a 2-contract position, your account loses $1,000 by the next morning's open. With a $4,000 margin balance that is a 25% drawdown of the posted collateral; if the move is 200 points you receive a margin call before lunch. Leverage works in both directions, and the daily settlement is real cash — not a paper number.

    2.3 The Futures Curve: Contango and Backwardation

    The price of the December crude contract is rarely the same as the price of the October contract, even when both reference the same barrel of West Texas Intermediate. The shape of that term structure is the most important thing a commodity-futures investor watches.

    [VISUAL: image/week39_contango_uso.png]

    • Contango: back-month price > front-month price > spot. The market is paying you to delay delivery. This is the "normal" shape for storable commodities — the back-month price has to compensate the seller for storage cost, financing cost, and the lost opportunity of selling spot today. Crude oil is in contango about 60-70% of the time historically. The chart above shows the canonical 2015-2016 oil-glut episode and the 2020 super-contango — back-month minus front-month spread blew out to $30/bbl in April 2020 when storage at Cushing OK ran out and the front-month CL contract famously settled at negative $37 at expiration.
    • Backwardation: spot > front-month > back-month. The market is paying you to take delivery now. This happens when there is an immediate physical shortage — the convenience yield of holding the actual barrel in your tank exceeds the cost of carry. Crude was deeply backwardated in 2022 when Russia invaded Ukraine and prompt-month physical traded $20+ above the 12-month contract.

    The relationship between curve shape and commodity-ETF performance is exact and unforgiving. USO, the largest oil ETF, holds rolling front-month CL futures. Every month it sells the expiring contract and buys the next one. In contango, that round-trip is a guaranteed loss — you sell the cheap front for the expensive back, and pay the spread every roll. The 2009-2020 USO chart is the single best illustration of this drag in financial history: spot WTI averaged roughly $60/bbl across that period, but USO compounded negatively at -8% per year. Contango ate roughly 4-6% per year, persistent and unrecoverable, year after year.

    The fix, if you actually want crude exposure: hold the December-2027 contract (or even further out), not the front month. The deferred contract has a flatter roll. Better yet, hold it in the futures account itself — no ETF wrapper, no expense ratio, and the §1256 tax treatment instead of the K-1 partnership tax form USO ships out every year.

    2.4 Section 1256: The 60/40 Rule

    This is the SOUL #15 capstone. Almost all retail-tradeable index futures (/ES, /MES, /NQ, /MNQ, /YM, /RTY, /CL, /MCL, /GC, /MGC) and a narrow set of broad-based index options (SPX, RUT, NDX) are classified under Section 1256 of the Internal Revenue Code. The treatment is unique and entirely favourable:

  • All gains are taxed 60% long-term, 40% short-term, regardless of holding period. A two-day swing trade in /MES is taxed at the same blended rate as a multi-year position. For a 32% ordinary / 15% LTCG investor, the blended rate is 0.60 × 15% + 0.40 × 32% = 21.8%. Compare to a short-term equity gain at 32%, or a single-stock option gain held under a year at 32%. The structural edge is roughly 10 percentage points per dollar.
  • Mark-to-market at year-end. All open §1256 positions are marked to market on December 31 and the unrealised P&L is reported on Form 6781 as if the positions were closed and reopened. This is the same treatment as the broker reports daily; you do not pick up a phantom gain you did not see.
  • Loss carryback. §1256 losses can be carried back three years (rare in tax) against §1256 gains, in addition to forward-carry. Equity capital losses can only carry forward.

    • For a worked example: a $20,000 gain in /MES held over six months. Under §1256, $12,000 is taxed at LTCG (15-20%) and $8,000 at ordinary (32-37%). Total tax at 32% bracket: $12,000 × 15% + $8,000 × 32% = $1,800 + $2,560 = $4,360, an effective rate of 21.8%. The same $20,000 gain on SPY held six months would be all short-term at 32% = $6,400. Difference: $2,040, or 10.2% of the gain itself. Over a career of active trading that compounds into real money, and it is the single biggest reason desks that trade index exposure structurally do it through futures rather than through ETFs.

    2.5 Roll Mechanics

    Futures expire. The active /MES contract rolls quarterly: March, June, September, December (the "front month" letters H, M, U, Z respectively). About a week before expiration, liquidity migrates from the expiring contract to the next quarterly. The roll itself is mechanical: sell the expiring contract, buy the next quarterly, in a single calendar-spread order that pays the (usually small, for index futures) basis differential.

    For equity-index futures the roll cost is structurally tiny — typically 5-15 basis points per quarter (driven by the cost-of-carry: short-rate minus dividend yield × 0.25). On /MES at 5,200, that is 2-8 index points. For an investor holding /MES as portable beta exposure, the all-in annual roll friction is roughly 0.20-0.60% per year — far cheaper than the LEAPS extrinsic (~2-3%/yr) or the SSO drag (~3-4%/yr).

    For commodities, roll cost depends entirely on curve shape. In contango, every roll is a loss (you saw this above for USO). In backwardation, every roll is a gain — which is exactly why commodity hedge funds prefer to be long oil only when the curve is backwardated. The signal is observable on the screen; you do not have to forecast it.

    3. Common Misconceptions

  • "Futures are gambling." Naked /ES at 50x leverage is gambling. Sized /MES at 1-3 contracts on a $100k account at 0.3-0.8x portfolio leverage is portable beta exposure. Same instrument family — same difference between a 0.30Δ weekly and a 0.92Δ LEAPS in the option world.
  • "Futures margin is a loan." It is not. It is a performance bond held by the exchange. There is no interest charge. The "leverage" is a function of position size relative to posted margin, not borrowing.
  • "Contango is a temporary thing." Crude has been in contango for roughly two-thirds of its history since 1983. It is the normal shape of a storable commodity curve. Backwardation is the rarer state.
  • "USO went to zero because of fraud." USO's underperformance is mechanical: roll yield in a chronically contangoed curve. No fraud, no fund-level mistake — the wrapper itself is structurally short the futures basis. Same with UNG (natural gas) and VXX (VIX).
  • "Section 1256 only applies if I hold for a year." False. Section 1256 60/40 applies regardless of holding period. A trade open for 30 minutes and closed at a profit is taxed identically to a position held three years. That is the entire point.
  • "I should use /ES because it's more liquid than /MES." /MES is plenty liquid (>1M contracts/day) for any retail-sized position. The "more liquid" argument matters when you need to move 1,000 contracts at once. For 1-10 contracts, /MES vs /ES is a tick or two of slippage difference, and the position-sizing flexibility of /MES is worth it.
  • "Futures don't pay dividends, so they're worse than stocks." The dividend is embedded in the price difference between the futures and the spot index. The futures curve discounts forward expected dividends; you do not lose them, you just receive them as price-return rather than cash.
  • "24-hour markets means I can trade anytime." Most off-hours liquidity is institutional hedging flow. Spreads widen 3-10x outside the 9:30-16:00 ET window. Trade in the cash session unless you have a specific reason not to.
  • "§1256 means I never owe ordinary tax on futures." False. 40% of §1256 gain is ordinary. The benefit is the 60% LTCG portion regardless of holding period — which on multi-year holds is worse than equity LTCG (where 100% would be long-term).
  • "A leveraged ETF is the same thing as a futures position." Wrong on three counts: ETFs reset daily and decay (week 37); ETF gains are taxed at equity rates with no §1256; ETF expense ratios are 0.7-1.0% drag while futures roll cost is 0.2-0.6%. Futures dominate for active rotation.

    • 4. Q&A Section

    Q1. How much account size do I need to trade /MES? Most brokers (TastyTrade, IBKR, Schwab Futures) require $2,000-5,000 minimum to enable futures trading and clear ~$2,000 initial margin per /MES contract. Practical floor is roughly $25,000 — that gives you room for 2-3 contracts plus the discipline buffer to absorb a 10% drawdown without margin call.

    Q2. What's the daily mark-to-market mean for my taxes? On the brokerage statement you see daily P&L sweeps. For tax purposes, only year-end positions are mark-to-market under §1256 (Form 6781). Closed trades are realised normally. The two reports reconcile.

    Q3. Can I trade futures in a Roth IRA? Yes — TastyTrade, IBKR, Schwab all offer futures-cleared IRAs. There is no margin call risk in an IRA the way there is in a regular margin account, but you cannot replenish a margin shortfall mid-day, so brokers typically require 1.5-2x normal margin in IRAs. The §1256 tax benefit is moot in an IRA (no tax on anything), but the leverage and execution are still useful.

    Q4. What's the difference between /MES and SPX (the index option)? SPX is a European-style cash-settled index option, also taxed under §1256. /MES is the futures contract. SPX is for option-strategy exposure (spreads, condors); /MES is for delta-1 directional exposure. Both are 60/40 taxed.

    Q5. How often should I roll /MES? On the Thursday before the third Friday of expiration month — that is when liquidity has migrated to the next quarterly contract. Your broker will warn you a week in advance. Do not let it expire; the cash settlement to the index print will lock in whatever the open print happens to be.

    Q6. What happens if I get a margin call on /MES? The broker will liquidate enough of your position to bring you back above maintenance margin, often the same day, often without warning. Futures margin calls are not the 30-day "send a check" calls of the equities world. Maintain at least 1.5x initial margin in your account and you will essentially never see one.

    Q7. Why is contango worse for some commodities than others? Storage cost dominates. Crude (storable, but expensive to store offshore) is moderately contangoed. Natural gas (very expensive to store, seasonal demand) is severely contangoed in summer. Metals (cheap to store) are mildly contangoed. Live cattle (impossible to store cheaply) is often backwardated. The cost-of-carry formula tells you the magnitude.

    Q8. Can I express a "long volatility" view through /MES? No — /MES is delta-1 on the index. For long-vol exposure you need /VX (VIX futures) or VIX options. /VX has its own contango problem (futures price > spot VIX about 80% of the time), which is why VXX bleeds 30-60%/year in normal markets.

    Q9. How does this fit with the four-tranche framework (SOUL #13)? /MES is a portable-beta lever for the L1 sleeve: when the 60/40 portfolio is at 55% equity and you want 65% for a quarter, post $4-6k of margin and add 2 /MES contracts. Cheaper than rebalancing into SPY (taxes), faster than waiting for the next contribution.

    Q10. What's a reasonable position size in /MES? A useful rule: size such that a 5% adverse move in the underlying index does not exceed 10% of your liquid net worth. For a $100k account that means roughly 4 /MES contracts max ($104k notional), and most active retail traders I respect run 1-2.

    Q11. Why does Horace mention SOUL #15 specifically here? Because §1256 60/40 is the cleanest tax treatment any retail US investor can get on directional speculation. You do not have to hold for a year. You do not have to harvest losses. You do not have to navigate wash-sale rules. The 21.8% blended rate at the 32% bracket is a structural gift from the 1981 tax code, and it has not been touched since — it is the single most under-appreciated thing in the US retail tax code.

    Q12. When does this strategy fail? Three regimes. (1) Over-leverage: sizing /MES based on margin posted instead of notional controlled — the classic blow-up. (2) Holding commodity futures long-term in contango: /MCL or /MNG decay through roll just like USO/UNG. (3) Off-hours trading on news: spreads widen 5x+ at 3 a.m. ET; the slippage on a 50-point /MES move during off-hours can exceed the move itself.

    7. 滾動收益率與期限結構

    滾動收益率是將到期期貨合約換倉至較後日期合約時所產生的回報（正或負）。這是期貨投資中最重要卻最少被理解的概念之一。 

    滾動收益率情境

    
正滾動收益率（期貨貼水）：
到期合約：$72（你持有好倉）
次月合約：$70

    

    
你以$72賣出，以$70買入
滾動收益率：每份合約 +$2

    

    
若期貨貼水持續，你僅從滾倉即可獲得溢價。
這種情況常見於供應中斷期間的能源市場。

    

    
負滾動收益率（期貨溢價）：
到期合約：$70（你持有好倉）
次月合約：$72

    

    
你以$70賣出，以$72買入
滾動收益率：每份合約 -$2

    

    
每個月僅因滾倉便虧損$2。
一年下來：每份合約 -$24（可能超過30%）
這正是長期商品交易所買賣基金往往跑輸的原因。

    

    
零滾動收益率（水平期限結構）：
實際上很少出現。發生於期貨曲線完全水平
（所有月份合約價格相同）的情況。


    滾動收益率對回報的影響

    
商品現貨回報（年化）： +10%
期限結構：              期貨溢價（每月滾倉成本2%）
年化滾動收益率：        -24%

    

    
期貨總回報：+10% - 24% = -14%

    

    
商品價格上升了10%，但你的期貨倉位
虧損了14%。這就是期貨溢價陷阱。

    

    
這正是USO（石油交易所買賣基金）在2020至2023年
大幅跑輸油價的原因。負滾動收益率蠶食了所有盈利，
甚至造成更大虧損。

    c) 常見誤解

    誤解一：「期貨保證金與股票保證金運作方式相同。」

    兩者截然不同。股票保證金是一種貸款——你向券商借錢購買更多股票，並須支付貸款利息。期貨保證金是一種履約保證金——用以確保你能履行合約義務的按金，無需支付利息，且槓桿比例遠高於股票（20:1或更高，而股票保證金僅為2:1）。這使期貨在資金效益上更高，但同時風險更大。

    誤解二：「期貨只適合投機者和商品交易員。」

    期貨雖起源於商品市場，並被投機者所使用，但同時也是對沖（航空公司對沖燃油成本、農民對沖農作物價格）、投資組合管理（退休基金調整股票敞口）及套利（機構維持跨市場價格一致）的重要工具。微型期貨更令散戶得以將期貨作為實際的投資組合管理工具。

    誤解三：「期貨溢價意味著市場預期價格將上升。」

    期貨溢價並不一定反映價格上升的預期。在大多數情況下，期貨溢價反映的是持倉成本：儲存成本、融資成本及保險費用。黃金期貨曲線呈期貨溢價，僅代表期貨價格涵蓋了將黃金儲存至交割日期的成本。市場在處於期貨溢價狀態的同時，仍可預期現貨價格下跌。

    誤解四：「期貨比期權風險更高。」

    這是一種過度簡化。期貨的盈虧是線性的，雙向風險均無上限。期權買方的風險有限，但賣方的風險無上限。以每美元保證金計算，期貨和期權的風險可以相當。關鍵分別在於：期貨虧損可超過初始保證金，而買入期權的虧損以所付期權金為上限。但期貨不受時間值損耗影響，令其在特定策略上更具優勢。

    誤解五：「我可以像持有股票一樣無限期持有期貨。」

    期貨有到期日。若你希望持續持有相關敞口，便須將到期合約換倉至下一份合約。此滾倉過程涉及實際成本（佣金、買賣差價及滾動收益率），長期累積下來相當可觀。你不能像持有股票般一買便了事。

    誤解六：「近月合約永遠是最佳交易選擇。」

    對短線交易而言，近月合約通常流動性最高。但對較長期的倉位而言，季度合約（三月、六月、九月、十二月）往往更為合適，因為滾倉頻率較低。此外，近月合約在臨近到期時可能出現波動加劇、流動性下降的情況，令遠月合約在合約到期前數天成為更穩妥的選擇。

    d) 常見問題解答

    問題一：入市期貨交易需要多少資金？

    答：對於微型期貨，建議每份合約最少備有$5,000。這能提供足夠的保證金，以及應對正常市場波動的緩衝。對於電子迷你合約，$25,000至$50,000是更為合適的起步金額，因為較大的合約規模需要更多資本進行妥善的風險管理。許多券商允許你以最低$2,000開立期貨賬戶，但這對持續交易而言遠遠不夠——一個壞日子便可能令賬戶爆倉。

    問題二：若我持有實物交割合約至到期日，會發生什麼？

    答：若你持有實物交割合約（如原油，CL）至到期日，你可能須接收實物商品。對於CL，這意味着須接收在俄克拉荷馬州庫欣交割的1,000桶石油。顯然，大多數散戶並不希望如此。務必在到期前平倉或滾倉——通常應在首次通知日（一般在最後交易日前數天）之前完成。若你未能及時處理，券商可能會強制平倉，並收取相關費用。

    問題三：期貨的60/40稅務規則如何運作？

    答：根據美國國稅局第1256條款，所有受規管期貨合約須於年底按市值計算。任何未變現盈虧均視作於12月31日平倉處理。盈利隨後以混合稅率徵稅：60%按長期資本增值稅率（目前為20%），40%按短期稅率（最高37%），與實際持倉時間無關。最高有效稅率約為26.8%，相較股票短線交易的37%低得多。你亦可將虧損向前追溯3年，抵銷過往的第1256條款盈利——這是標準資本虧損所不具備的選項。

    問題四：電子迷你合約與微型合約有何分別？

    答：微型期貨的規模恰好是電子迷你合約的十分之一。微型標普500電子迷你（MES）的乘數為每點$5，而電子迷你（ES）則為$50。因此MES的合約價值約為$25,000，ES則約為$250,000，保證金亦按比例相應調低。微型期貨採用與電子迷你版本相同的交易時間、到期日及結算方式，非常適合資金規模較小的賬戶及需要精細調整倉位規模的投資者。

    問題五：如何進行期貨滾倉？

    答：滾倉即平掉當前合約，並在較後日期的合約建立新倉位。你可以分兩步操作（賣出到期合約，買入下一份），或透過許多券商提供的「日曆差價」訂單同時執行兩個方向，這樣效率更高。滾倉時機因合約而異——股票指數交易者通常在到期週前的週四滾倉（稱為「滾倉日」），屆時流動性會轉移至下一份合約。大多數活躍交易者在到期前一至兩週滾倉。

    問題六：能否在個人退休賬戶中交易期貨？

    答：可以，但有所限制。許多期貨券商提供允許期貨交易的個人退休賬戶。然而，你不能以傳統的保證金方式使用槓桿——保證金須由賬戶餘額全額覆蓋。部分券商會限制個人退休賬戶可交易的期貨品種。由於退休賬戶的盈利已享有遞延或免稅待遇，60/40稅務規則不適用於個人退休賬戶，因此期貨的稅務優勢在退休賬戶中無法體現。

    問題七：為何商品交易所買賣基金跑輸現貨價格？

    答：使用期貨的商品交易所買賣基金（如USO石油交易所買賣基金或UNG天然氣交易所買賣基金）受制於滾動收益率。當期貨市場處於期貨溢價狀態（期貨價格 > 現貨價格）時，交易所買賣基金須賣出較便宜的到期合約，買入較昂貴的遠期合約，每次滾倉均蒙受損失。長期累積下來，這種「期貨溢價拖累」可以非常巨大。USO自成立以來已損失逾90%的價值，而油價的跌幅卻相對有限。這種結構性拖累令長期持有持續處於期貨溢價狀態市場的商品交易所買賣基金的問題相當嚴重。

    問題八：期貨與隔夜股市走勢有何關係？

    答：股票指數期貨（ES、NQ）近乎全天候交易，而股票市場每日只有6.5小時的交易時段。當你看到股市在開市時出現「跳空高開」或「跳空低開」，期貨市場早已在那個價位交易了數個小時。隔夜期貨交易反映了突發新聞、國際市場走勢及經濟數據公布的影響。許多專業交易員在股市開市前會密切留意期貨走勢，以判斷當天的大方向。

    閱讀單元

    a) 為什麼這很重要

    期貨市場是全球最古老、流動性最高的金融市場之一，然而大多數散戶投資人從未學習過其運作方式。這是金融知識上的重大缺口，原因如下：

    • 期貨主導價格發現：許多資產——石油、黃金、利率、股票指數水準——都是先在期貨市場定價，再反映至現貨市場。了解期貨，有助於理解全球價格的形成機制。
    • 期貨是機構交易的骨幹：退休基金、避險基金及大宗商品生產商都廣泛使用期貨。當你聽到「機構資金流入股市」，這些資金流動很大一部分是透過期貨市場進行的。
    • 微型期貨為散戶打開了大門：自2019年起，芝商所（CME）推出了微型期貨合約，規模僅為標準合約的十分之一，首次讓個人投資人能夠以實際可行的方式參與期貨市場。
    • 期貨具備獨特優勢：近乎24小時交易、稅務優惠（60/40法則）、無模式當沖交易限制，以及高效的槓桿，使期貨成為特定策略的有力工具。
    • 理解期貨升水與逆價差：這些期貨市場的概念，解釋了為何許多熱門指數股票型基金（商品型指數股票型基金、波動率指數股票型基金）長期表現遜於其追蹤的標的資產。若不了解期貨運作機制，便無法正確評估這些產品。

    本課程將全面介紹期貨市場——合約如何運作、保證金如何運作、期貨價格與現貨市場的關係，以及如何將期貨納入精緻的投資工具組合。

    b) 你需要了解的內容

    1. 期貨合約基礎

    期貨合約是一種具有法律約束力的協議，約定雙方在特定未來日期以預定價格買賣特定資產。雙方均有義務履行合約（不同於選擇權，買方擁有選擇權利）。

    • 多頭部位：同意以合約價格買入。價格上漲時獲利。
    • 空頭部位：同意以合約價格賣出。價格下跌時獲利。
    • 合約月份：合約到期的月份（大多數金融期貨為3月、6月、9月、12月）。
    • 近月合約：最近到期的合約（流動性最高）。
    • 遠月合約：到期日較晚的合約。
    • 未平倉量：尚未了結的合約總數。
    • 結算：合約到期時的處置方式——以實物交割資產，或以現金支付差額。

    實物交割與現金結算： 

    實物交割（例如：原油期貨——CL）：

    
到期時，空方交付實物商品，
多方支付合約價格。

可能須承擔接收1,000桶石油的義務！
務必在到期前平倉。

到期時，以現金支付合約價格
與結算價格之間的差額。

你收到：(5,050 - 5,000) × $50 = $2,500
無須實物交割。

    2. 期貨保證金：初始保證金、維持保證金與變動保證金

    變動保證金（逐日盯市）：

    期貨部位每日逐日盯市。這意味著盈虧在每個交易日結束時以現金結算，而非在平倉時結算。 

    逐日盯市範例

    
第0日：開多1口ES，進場價5,000點。存入初始保證金$12,500。

    

    
第1日：ES收盤5,020點（+20點）
當日損益：+20 × $50 = +$1,000
帳戶餘額：$12,500 + $1,000 = $13,500

    

    
第2日：ES收盤4,980點（較第1日下跌40點）
當日損益：-40 × $50 = -$2,000
帳戶餘額：$13,500 - $2,000 = $11,500

    

    
$11,500 > $11,300維持保證金：無須追繳

    

    
第3日：ES收盤4,960點（較第2日下跌20點）
當日損益：-20 × $50 = -$1,000
帳戶餘額：$11,500 - $1,000 = $10,500

    

    
$10,500 < $11,300維持保證金：追繳保證金通知！
須存入$2,000以恢復至初始保證金（$12,500）
若未於截止時間前存入：部位將被強制平倉

    

    
第4日：存入$2,000。帳戶餘額：$12,500
ES收盤5,010點（+50點）
當日損益：+50 × $50 = $2,500
帳戶餘額：$12,500 + $2,500 = $15,000

    

    
從進場（5,000點）至第4日收盤（5,010點）總損益：
+10 × $50 = +$500
帳戶餘額確認：$12,500初始 + $2,000追繳 + $500損益 = $15,000

    c) 常見迷思

    迷思一：「期貨保證金的運作方式與股票保證金相同。」

    兩者根本上截然不同。股票保證金是向券商借入的資金，你需要支付利息。期貨保證金是一種履約擔保金——確保你能履行義務的存款。期貨保證金不需支付利息，且槓桿比率遠高於股票保證金（20:1以上，相比股票保證金的2:1）。這使得期貨既更具資金效益，同時也更具風險。

    迷思二：「期貨只適用於投機者和大宗商品交易人。」

    雖然期貨起源於大宗商品市場且被投機者廣泛使用，但它們也是避險（航空公司對燃油成本進行避險、農民對農作物價格進行避險）、投資組合管理（退休基金調整股票曝險）以及套利（機構維持跨市場價格一致性）的重要工具。微型期貨也讓散戶在投資組合管理中實際使用期貨成為可能。

    迷思三：「期貨升水意味著市場預期價格將上漲。」

    期貨升水不一定反映市場對價格上漲的預期。在大多數情況下，期貨升水反映的是持有成本：倉儲成本、融資成本和保險費用。黃金期貨曲線呈現升水，僅表示期貨價格包含了存放黃金至交割日的成本。市場可以處於期貨升水狀態，同時預期現貨價格下跌。

    迷思四：「期貨比選擇權風險更高。」

    這是過度簡化的說法。期貨的損益是線性且雙向無限的。選擇權買方的風險有限，而賣方的風險則無限。以每元保證金計算，期貨和選擇權的風險程度可以相當。關鍵差異在於：期貨的虧損可以超過初始保證金，而持有選擇權多頭的虧損上限為所支付的權利金。但期貨也不受時間價值耗損影響，這使其在某些策略中更具優勢。

    迷思五：「我可以像持有股票一樣長期持有期貨。」

    期貨有到期日。如果你想持續持有曝險，必須將部位從到期合約轉倉至下一個合約。這個轉倉過程有實際成本（手續費、買賣價差及滾動收益率），長期累積下來相當可觀。你無法像持有股票那樣買入後置之不理。

    迷思六：「近月合約永遠是最適合交易的合約。」

    對於短線交易，近月合約通常流動性最高。但對於較長期的部位，季月合約（3月、6月、9月、12月）往往更為合適，因為轉倉頻率較低。此外，近月合約在到期前可能因流動性降低而波動加劇，使得遠月合約在合約到期前最後幾天成為更安全的選擇。

    d) 常見問答

    Q1：我需要多少資金才能開始交易期貨？

    A：對於微型期貨，建議每口交易合約至少備有$5,000。這能提供足夠的保證金加上應對正常市場波動的緩衝空間。對於電子迷你合約，$25,000至$50,000是更適合的起始資金，因為較大的合約規模需要更多資本進行妥善的風險管理。許多券商允許以低至$2,000開設期貨帳戶，但這對持續交易而言並不充裕——一個糟糕的交易日就可能清光帳戶。

    Q2：若我持有實物交割合約至到期日會怎樣？

    A：若你持有實物交割合約（如原油CL）至到期日，可能須承擔接收實物商品的義務。對於CL而言，這意味著須接收交割至奧克拉荷馬州庫欣的1,000桶石油。顯然，大多數散戶交易人不希望如此。務必在到期日之前平倉或轉倉實物交割合約——通常在「首次通知日」之前，該日期一般在最後交易日前幾天。若你未主動處理，券商可能會強制平倉，並可能收取相關費用。

    Q3：期貨的60/40稅務法則如何運作？

    A：根據美國稅法第1256條款，所有受監管的期貨合約均須在年底逐日盯市。任何未實現的盈虧均視同於12月31日平倉處理。盈虧隨後以混合稅率課稅：60%按長期資本利得稅率（目前為20%）課稅，40%按短期稅率（最高37%）課稅，無論你實際持有部位多久。這使最高實際稅率降至約26.8%，相較於股票短期交易的37%更為優惠。你還可以將虧損往前3年抵減，以沖抵先前的第1256條款獲利——這是一般資本利損所沒有的選項。

    Q4：電子迷你期貨與微型期貨有何差異？

    A：微型期貨恰好是電子迷你合約的十分之一大小。微型電子迷你標普500（MES）的每點乘數為$5，而電子迷你（ES）為$50。因此MES的合約價值約為$25,000，ES則約為$250,000，保證金也按比例較低。微型期貨採用與電子迷你版本相同的交易時間、到期日和結算方式。它們非常適合較小規模的帳戶，以及需要精細調整部位規模的交易人。

    Q5：如何進行期貨轉倉？

    A：轉倉是指平倉目前的合約，同時在較遠期的合約開立新部位。你可以分兩筆交易進行（賣出到期合約、買入下一個合約），或者許多券商提供「價差委託單」，同時執行兩腿，效率更高。轉倉時機因合約而異——股票指數交易人通常在到期週前一個週四（稱為「轉倉日」）進行轉倉，此時流動性開始轉移至下一個合約。大多數積極交易人在到期日前1至2週進行轉倉。

    Q6：我可以在個人退休帳戶（IRA）中交易期貨嗎？

    A：可以，但有所限制。許多期貨券商提供允許期貨交易的退休帳戶。然而，你無法以傳統保證金貸款的方式使用槓桿——帳戶餘額必須覆蓋全額保證金要求。部分券商限制退休帳戶只能交易特定期貨產品。60/40稅務法則不適用於退休帳戶（因為帳戶內的獲利本已享有遞延課稅或免稅優惠），因此在退休帳戶中，期貨的稅務優勢會消失。

    Q7：為什麼商品型指數股票型基金的表現遜於現貨價格？

    A：使用期貨的商品型指數股票型基金（如追蹤石油的USO或追蹤天然氣的UNG）會受到滾動收益率的影響。當期貨市場處於期貨升水狀態（期貨價格 > 現貨價格）時，指數股票型基金必須賣出較便宜的到期合約，並買入較昂貴的遠月合約，每次轉倉都會虧損。長期累積下來，這種「期貨升水拖累」可能造成巨大損失。以USO為例，自成立以來已虧損逾90%的淨值，而油價僅小幅下滑。在持續期貨升水的市場中，這種結構性拖累使得長期持有商品型指數股票型基金問題重重。

    Q8：期貨與股市隔夜波動有何關聯？

    A：股票指數期貨（ES、NQ）每天近乎24小時交易，而股票市場僅交易6.5小時。當你看到股市在開盤時「跳空上漲」或「跳空下跌」，期貨市場早已在這些價位交易了數小時。隔夜期貨交易反映了突發新聞、國際市場走勢以及經濟數據公告。許多專業交易人會在股市開盤前觀察期貨，以研判當日市場的可能方向。

    1. 期货合约基础

    期货合约是一种具有法律约束力的协议，约定在未来某一特定日期以预定价格买入或卖出特定资产。合约双方均有义务履约（与期权不同，期权的买方拥有选择权）。 

    期货合约要素解析

    
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   期货合约                           │
│                                                      │
│  标的资产：标准普尔500指数                          │
│  合约代码：ES（E-mini标准普尔500）                  │
│  合约规模：$50 × 指数点位                           │
│  最小变动价位：0.25指数点（$12.50）                 │
│  到期日：合约月份第三个星期五                       │
│  结算方式：现金结算                                 │
│  交易时间：周日下午6点至周五下午5点（东部时间，     │
│            近24小时）                               │
│  交易所：CME（芝加哥商品交易所）                   │
│                                                      │
│  若标准普尔500指数位于5,000点：                     │
│  合约价值 = $50 × 5,000 = $250,000                  │
│  初始保证金 = 约$12,500（合约价值的5%）             │
│  杠杆倍数 = 20:1                                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    核心术语：

    • 多头头寸：同意以合约价格买入。价格上涨时获利。
    • 空头头寸：同意以合约价格卖出。价格下跌时获利。
    • 合约月份：合约到期的月份（大多数金融期货为3月、6月、9月、12月）。
    • 近月合约：最近到期的合约（流动性最强）。
    • 远月合约：到期日较晚的合约。
    • 持仓量：未平仓合约的总数量。
    • 结算：合约到期时的了结方式——或以实物交割资产，或以现金支付差额。

    实物交割与现金结算： 

    实物交割（例如：原油期货——CL）：

    
到期时，空头交付实物大宗商品，
多头按合约价格付款。

    

    
警告：若持有实物交割合约至到期日，
你可能需要接收1,000桶原油！务必在到期前平仓。

    

    
现金结算（例如：标准普尔500期货——ES）：
到期时，以现金支付合约价格与结算价格之间的差额。

    

    
示例：你持有ES多头，建仓价5,000。结算价为5,050。
你收到：(5,050 - 5,000) × $50 = $2,500
无需进行实物交割。

    变动保证金（逐日盯市）：

    期货头寸每日按市值计价。这意味着盈亏在每个交易日结束时以现金结算，而非等到平仓时结算。 

    逐日盯市示例

    
第0天：以5,000价格开1手ES多头，缴纳$12,500初始保证金。

当日盈亏：+20 × $50 = +$1,000
账户余额：$12,500 + $1,000 = $13,500

    

    
第2天：ES收盘4,980（较第1天-40点）
当日盈亏：-40 × $50 = -$2,000
账户余额：$13,500 - $2,000 = $11,500

    

    
$11,500 > $11,300维持保证金：无需追加保证金

    

    
第3天：ES收盘4,960（较第2天-20点）
当日盈亏：-20 × $50 = -$1,000
账户余额：$11,500 - $1,000 = $10,500

    

    
$10,500 < $11,300维持保证金：触发追加保证金通知！
须存入$2,000以恢复至初始保证金水平（$12,500）
若未在截止时间前存入：头寸将被强制平仓

    

    
第4天：你存入$2,000，账户余额：$12,500
ES收盘5,010（+50点）
当日盈亏：+50 × $50 = $2,500
账户余额：$12,500 + $2,500 = $15,000

    

    
从建仓（5,000）至第4天收盘（5,010）的总盈亏：
+10 × $50 = +$500
账户余额验证：$12,500初始 + $2,000追加 + $500 = $15,000
但你需要额外注入资金才能撑过回撤期。

    7. 展期收益率与期限结构

    展期收益率是指将到期期货合约展期至远月合约时产生的收益（正或负）。这是期货投资中最重要、也最容易被忽视的概念之一。 

    展期收益率情景

    
正展期收益率（期货贴水）：
到期合约：$72（你持有多头）
次月合约：$70

    

    
你以$72卖出，以$70买入
展期收益率：每手 +$2

    

    
若贴水状态持续，仅凭展期便可赚取溢价。
这在供应中断期间的能源市场中较为常见。

    

    
负展期收益率（期货升水）：
到期合约：$70（你持有多头）
次月合约：$72

    

    
你以$70卖出，以$72买入
展期收益率：每手 -$2

    

    
每月仅因展期便损失$2。
一年累计：每手 -$24（潜在损失30%以上）
这就是大宗商品交易所交易基金长期跑输的原因。

    

    
零展期收益率（水平期限结构）：
实践中极为罕见。出现于期货曲线完全平坦
（所有月份价格相同）时。


    展期收益率对总收益的影响

    
大宗商品现货年收益率：  +10%
期限结构：               期货升水（每月展期成本2%）
年度展期收益率：         -24%

    

    
期货总收益率：+10% - 24% = -14%

    

    
大宗商品价格上涨了10%，但你的期货头寸
却亏损了14%。这就是期货升水陷阱。

    

    
这也是USO（石油交易所交易基金）在2020-2023年
大幅跑输油价的原因——负展期收益率吞噬了全部收益乃至更多。

    10. 期货交易风险管理

    期货仓位管理

    
原则：每笔交易的风险不超过账户资金的1%-2%

    

    
账户规模：$50,000
每笔交易风险：1% = $500

    

    
MES（微型标普500）：
每点价值：$5
可承受点数：$500 / $5 = 100点
标普位于5,000：100 / 5,000 = 2%止损距离
最大手数：1手MES（100点止损）

    

    
ES（E-mini标普500）：
每点价值：$50
可承受点数：$500 / $50 = 10点
标普位于5,000：10 / 5,000 = 0.2%止损距离
此止损极为严苛——不建议在$50,000账户上使用

    

    
$50,000账户交易ES：
至少需要30-50点止损 = 风险$1,500-2,500
占账户资金3%-5%——风险过大

    

    
结论：$50,000账户应交易MES而非ES。
ES需要至少$200,000以上的账户才能进行合理的风险管理。

    

    
杠杆使用准则：
保守型：已用保证金 < 账户资金的10%
适中型：已用保证金 < 账户资金的25%
激进型：已用保证金 < 账户资金的50%

    

    
保证金使用率永远不要超过50%。
黑天鹅事件（闪崩、跌停）可能导致止损失效。

    c) 常见误解

    误解一："期货保证金与股票保证金的原理相同。"

    两者完全不同。股票保证金是一种贷款——你向券商借钱购买更多股票，并为借款支付利息。期货保证金是一种履约保证金——确保你有能力履行义务的押金。期货保证金无需支付利息，且杠杆比例远高于股票保证金（20:1或更高，而股票保证金仅为2:1）。这使期货既更具资金效率，也更具风险。

    误解二："期货只适合投机者和大宗商品交易者。"

    期货虽然起源于大宗商品市场并被投机者广泛使用，但它也是对冲（航空公司对冲燃油成本、农民对冲农产品价格）、投资组合管理（养老基金调整股票敞口）以及套利（机构维持跨市场价格一致性）的重要工具。微型期货更是让期货成为散户进行投资组合管理的实用工具。

    误解三："期货升水意味着市场预期价格将上涨。"

    期货升水并不必然反映市场对价格上涨的预期。在大多数情况下，期货升水反映的是持有成本：仓储费、融资成本和保险费。黄金期货曲线呈期货升水，仅意味着期货价格包含了持有黄金至交割日的仓储成本。市场可以处于期货升水状态，同时预期现货价格下跌。

    误解四："期货比期权风险更大。"

    这是一种过度简化。期货在双方向均具有线性、无限风险。期权买方的风险有限，卖方的风险则无限。从每美元保证金的角度来看，期货和期权的风险可能不相上下。关键区别在于：期货的亏损可能超过初始保证金，而做多期权的最大亏损限于所支付的期权费。但期货没有时间衰减，这使其在某些策略中更具优势。

    误解五："我可以像持有股票一样无限期持有期货。"

    期货会到期。若想保持持续敞口，须将到期合约展期至下一份合约。这一展期过程产生真实成本（手续费、买卖价差和展期收益率），长期累计可能相当可观。你无法像持有股票一样买入后置之不理。

    误解六："近月合约永远是最佳交易选择。"

    短期交易通常以近月合约流动性最强为宜。但对于较长期的头寸，季度合约（3月、6月、9月、12月）往往更为合适，因为展期频率更低。此外，近月合约在临近到期时可能出现波动加剧、流动性下降的情况，使远月合约在合约最后几天成为更安全的选择。

    d) 常见问题解答

    问题一：交易期货需要多少启动资金？

    答：微型期货建议每手合约至少配置$5,000资金。这样可以在保证金之外留有足够缓冲，应对正常市场波动。E-mini合约的更合适起点是$25,000至$50,000，因为更大的合约规模需要更多资金进行合理的风险管理。许多券商允许以低至$2,000开立期货账户，但这对持续交易来说是远远不够的——一个糟糕的交易日就可能让账户归零。

    问题二：如果我持有实物交割合约至到期日会发生什么？

    答：若持有实物交割合约（如原油CL）至到期日，你可能需要接收实物大宗商品。对于CL而言，这意味着将有1,000桶原油运抵俄克拉荷马州库欣。显然，大多数散户投资者并不希望发生这种情况。务必在到期前充分提前平仓或展期实物交割合约——通常是在首次通知日之前，该日期一般比最后交易日提前数天。若你未能主动操作，券商可能会强制平仓，并收取相应费用。

    问题三：期货的六四税收规则是如何运作的？

    答：根据美国国税局《税法》第1256条，所有受监管期货合约须在年末按市值计价。任何未实现的盈亏均视同于12月31日平仓处理。此后，收益按综合税率征税：60%按长期资本利得税率（目前为20%），40%按短期税率（最高37%），无论实际持有时间长短。这使最高有效税率约为26.8%，而股票短期交易的税率高达37%。此外，亏损还可向前追溯三年，抵扣此前的第1256条收益——这是标准资本亏损所不具备的优惠。

    问题四：E-mini期货与微型期货有何区别？

    答：微型期货的规模恰好是E-mini对应合约的十分之一。微型E-mini标普500（MES）的每点乘数为$5，而E-mini（ES）为$50。这意味着MES的合约价值约为$25,000，ES约为$250,000，保证金也按比例降低。微型期货与E-mini版本使用完全相同的交易时段、到期日和结算方式。它们非常适合小规模账户，也便于精细调整仓位大小。

    问题五：如何展期期货头寸？

    答：展期是指平掉当前到期合约，同时在远月合约中建立新头寸。你可以分两笔交易操作（卖出到期合约，买入下一份合约），也可以通过许多券商提供的"跨期价差"订单同时执行两条腿，效率更高。展期时机因合约而异——股指期货交易者通常在到期周的前一个星期四展期（称为"展期日"），因为届时流动性会向下一份合约转移。大多数活跃交易者会在到期日前1至2周展期。

    问题六：能否在个人退休账户（IRA）中交易期货？

    答：可以，但有所限制。许多期货券商提供允许期货交易的个人退休账户。但你无法以传统融资方式使用杠杆——账户余额必须能够覆盖全部保证金要求。部分券商限制个人退休账户可交易的期货品种。六四税收规则在个人退休账户中不适用（因为收益已享受递延或免税待遇），因此期货的税收优惠在退休账户中无法体现。

    问题七：为何大宗商品交易所交易基金跑输现货价格？

    答：使用期货的大宗商品交易所交易基金（如石油类的USO、天然气类的UNG）受制于展期收益率。当期货市场处于期货升水状态（期货价格 > 现货价格）时，交易所交易基金必须以较低价格卖出到期合约，以较高价格买入远月合约，每次展期都产生损失。长期累计下来，这种"期货升水拖累"可能极为惊人。例如，USO自成立以来已损失逾90%的价值，而同期油价跌幅却相当有限。这一结构性拖累使持续性期货升水市场中的大宗商品交易所交易基金长期持有极为不利。

    问题八：期货与股票的隔夜波动有何关联？

    答：股指期货（ES、NQ）近乎全天候24小时交易，而股市每天仅交易6.5小时。当你看到股市在开盘时跳空高开或跳空低开，期货市场早在数小时前便已在那些价位运行。隔夜期货交易反映了突发新闻、国际市场动态和经济数据发布。许多专业交易者会在开市前观察期货走势，以研判当日行情方向。
