Slide r and g and watch the year-by-year NPV bars stretch and shrink. The terminal-value box on the right is the Gordon growth model — the same formula equity analysts use to price the entire perpetuity of future cash flows. The side bar chart shows what +100 bps of rate change does to four standard cash-flow streams: a 10-year bond, a 30-year bond, a long-duration growth equity, and a short-duration value equity. Notice the 30-year bond and the growth equity move by roughly the same amount — duration is duration whether the cash flow is labelled "bond" or "stock."
