# Review: course/level3_overview.md

## Overall Assessment

The Level 3 overview gives a clear map of the options and fixed-income block. It positions options as practical tools rather than gambling instruments, which is the right teaching instinct.

It needs stronger risk language, course-format cleanup, and SOUL alignment. The current version makes options income sound too consistent and fixed income too stabilizing without enough caveat.

## Content Critique

- The level map is clear and correctly groups Weeks 25-30 and 31-36.
- `Options as enhanced order types` is a useful hook, but it should be softened. Cash-secured puts and covered calls resemble limit orders only under specific assumptions; they add path dependency, volatility exposure, assignment risk, and tax complexity.
- `Write covered calls and cash-secured puts to generate consistent income` overpromises. Options generate premium, not guaranteed income; bad entries or volatility shocks can overwhelm the premium.
- Fixed income should not be introduced as pure stability. Duration, inflation, credit risk, and 2022-style rate shocks need a one-sentence caveat.
- The overview should mention that Level 3 is about controlled expression and sizing, not maximizing yield.
- Advanced financial statement analysis sits awkwardly under the `Fixed Income` block; it should be explained as credit/equity quality work bridging both sides.

## Structure And Formatting Issues

- Top-level reading sections are not numbered according to the course convention.
- The page lacks the standard four-part reading structure used by lessons.
- Add a level roadmap visual: options arc, fixed-income arc, and Week 36 integration.

## YouTube Script Critique

The script is friendly and accessible, especially Stella's fear of options.

Specific issues:

- Host description says `retail trader`; repository guidance says use `retail investor`.
- Horace's limit-order analogy needs a caveat, even in the overview.
- `The fear disappears` is too strong. Better: the fear becomes measurable.
- `Portfolio that actually pays you every month` could attract yield-chasing; pair it with `without pretending cash flow is free`.

## Chart And Visual Feedback

Recommended visual:

- A two-track roadmap: Options precision tools and fixed-income risk tools converging into Week 36 income portfolio.
- A warning strip: premium, duration, credit, tax, and assignment are the five risks Level 3 teaches you to measure.

## SOUL Consistency Flags

- Needs options-as-expression-toolkit framing.
- Avoid implying options are primarily income machines.
- Replace `retail trader` with `retail investor`.
- Add the post-2022 caveat that bonds can fail as shock absorbers when inflation and rates dominate.
