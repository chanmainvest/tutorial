# Review: course/faq.md

## Overall Assessment

The FAQ is candid and human, but it currently reads more like an author's note than a learner-support FAQ. It needs stronger trust-building, legal boundary language, and a cleaner order around what new students actually need to know.

## Content Critique

- The private tutoring answer is too blunt and transactional: `falls below a viable rate for the instructor` may sound dismissive. Better: `I am not able to offer private tutoring at this time`.
- The Patreon answer should explicitly say community Q&A is educational and not personalized financial advice.
- The MIT License answer is useful, but this is an audience-facing course page. Consider moving license detail to a footer/legal page or keeping it shorter.
- The `AI assistance` and `tokens were already spent` explanation is honest but weakens trust. Learners need to hear that the author reviewed, edited, and took responsibility for the material.
- The `Why did you really create this course?` answer has personality, but it leans too self-focused: YouTube peers, subscriber income, Bloomberg terminal, publisher solicitation. That may hurt credibility for students deciding whether to trust investment education.
- Add missing practical FAQ entries: who the course is for, prerequisites, whether it is financial advice, whether the course is US-focused, how to use side lessons, how much money someone needs to start, and whether options/futures lessons are required.
- Add a clear note that certification content is used as study inspiration/reference mapping, not official CFA Institute material or endorsement.

## Presentation Critique

Recommended order:

1. What is this course?
2. Who is it for?
3. Is this financial advice?
4. How should I use the 52-week structure?
5. Do I need options/futures/advanced tools?
6. Is it US-focused?
7. Where can I ask questions?
8. Can I reuse the materials?
9. Why did you create it?

The current order starts with private tutoring, which is not the strongest first impression.

## YouTube / Growth Feedback

For conversion and trust:

- Keep the honest founder voice, but make it learner-centered.
- The Bloomberg-terminal joke can work in a YouTube channel bio, but the FAQ should prioritize credibility.
- Add one concise invitation to YouTube/Patreon without making the course feel like a funnel.

## SOUL Consistency Flags

- The FAQ should mention the course's specific retail-investor mission: professional rigor plus retail advantages and constraints.
- It should avoid making the course sound like AI-generated notes. Horace's experience and philosophy are the differentiator.
