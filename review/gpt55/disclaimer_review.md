# Review: course/disclaimer.md

## Overall Assessment

The disclaimer is clear, sober, and mostly fit for purpose. It establishes the core educational-only boundary and covers performance, risk, third-party references, and user responsibility.

It should be expanded for the actual course surface: options selling, futures margin, structured products, volatility products, leveraged ETFs, tax jurisdiction, and no adviser-client relationship.

## Content Critique

- The educational-only disclaimer is strong.
- `No Professional Advice` is clear, but add explicit `no adviser-client relationship, fiduciary relationship, or duty is created by using the course`.
- The options risk disclosure is incomplete. It mentions premium loss, but the course also covers covered calls, cash-secured puts, spreads, undefined-risk examples, and volatility selling. Add assignment, early exercise, margin, liquidity, and losses beyond premium for option sellers.
- The futures disclosure should state that losses can exceed initial margin and that margin calls/liquidation can occur.
- Add explicit disclosures for structured products, volatility-linked products, leveraged/inverse ETFs, crypto, and margin borrowing.
- Add a jurisdiction caveat: tax rules, product access, investor protections, and regulations vary by country; much of the course appears US-market centered.
- `Stock market investments can lose value, including the entire amount invested` is true for individual stocks but broad-market funds rarely go literally to zero. Consider distinguishing individual securities from diversified funds.
- Third-party references should also say broker/platform screenshots or examples do not imply suitability.
- If Patreon/YouTube monetization exists, the disclaimer could add that educational-community revenue is separate from product compensation and does not make examples recommendations.

## Presentation Critique

- The page is readable and not overly hostile.
- Consider adding a short plain-English summary at the top before the legal sections: `This course teaches frameworks; you remain responsible for decisions.`
- Legal pages do not need the lesson decimal numbering convention, but consistent headings would still improve site polish.

## SOUL Consistency Flags

- The disclaimer supports the course's retail-investor discipline.
- It should explicitly warn that advanced tools are not necessary or suitable for every investor, even if taught in the course.
