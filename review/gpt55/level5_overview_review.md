# Review: course/level5_overview.md

## Overall Assessment

The Level 5 overview correctly positions the final six weeks as the capstone block. It gives a clear map from tail risk to structured products, volatility, factor tilts, managed futures, and portfolio integration.

It needs major tone softening. The current wording promises mastery, exploitation, professional-grade money management, and crisis-alpha profits too freely. The actual lessons are more nuanced and often warn against retail overimplementation.

## Content Critique

- The six-week map is clear and matches Weeks 47-52.
- `Master the most advanced portfolio strategies` is too strong. The course can introduce and integrate them; mastery requires live-cycle experience.
- `Techniques used by endowment funds, family offices, and top-tier portfolio managers` is directionally true but should emphasize retail adaptation and constraints.
- `Hedge your portfolio against extreme market events without excessive cost` overpromises. Tail hedges always have cost and implementation risk.
- `Exploit volatility term structure for additional returns` does not match Week 49's actual focus on IV/RV, VRP, and gamma scalping. Also `exploit` is too aggressive for retail education.
- `Crisis alpha that profits when everything else crashes` overstates managed futures. Trend pays on sustained trends, not every crash.
- Week 52 is more accurately IPS/portfolio integration than simply `all-weather portfolio`.
- The overview should explicitly say many Level 5 products are things students should understand in order to avoid or size small, not necessarily use.

## Structure And Formatting Issues

- Top-level reading sections are not numbered according to the course convention.
- Add a capstone roadmap visual with `understand`, `avoid`, `size`, and `integrate` columns.

## YouTube Script Critique

The script is enthusiastic and gives a satisfying endgame feeling.

Specific issues:

- Host description says `retail trader`; use `retail investor`.
- `Fifty-one weeks ago` is wrong for a Level 5 overview that appears before Week 47. Use `when this course began` or `back in Week 1`.
- `Volatility risk premium is one of the most reliable sources of return` needs the heavy-tail caveat in the same breath.
- `From zero to professional in 52 weeks` overclaims. Better: `from zero to a professional-style framework`.
- Week 52 should be described as an IPS/governance capstone, not only an all-weather allocation.

## Chart And Visual Feedback

Recommended visual:

- Level 5 map: Tail hedge, structured products, VRP, factors, CTA, IPS integration.
- A `what to use / what to avoid` matrix for Level 5 tools.

## SOUL Consistency Flags

- Strong topic alignment with SOUL: tail hedges, stores of value, VRP, CTA, and barbell integration.
- Needs better caution that advanced tools are expression instruments, not badges of sophistication.
- Replace trader wording and professional-overclaim language.
