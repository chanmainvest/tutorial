# CFA Program Curriculum Knowledge Map for an Experienced Retail Trader

## Executive summary

The CFA Program is structured to move you from **knowing tools** (Level I), to **applying valuation and analysis in context** (Level II), to **integrating everything into portfolio decisions under real-world constraints** (Level III). CFA Institute itself frames the levels as ÔÇťLearn and describeÔÇŁ (Level I), ÔÇťAnalyze and evaluateÔÇŁ (Level II), and ÔÇťIntegrate and applyÔÇŁ (Level III). ţłÇciteţłéturn5view0ţłéturn6view0ţłéturn7view0ţłü

For an experienced retail trader, the biggest ÔÇťgap versus a professionalÔÇŁ usually is not market familiarity; it is **professional-grade rigor**: (a) consistent return/risk measurement and performance interpretation (time-weighted vs money-weighted, attribution, benchmark thinking), (b) fixed-income and credit math (curves, duration/convexity, spread decomposition, structured products), (c) no-arbitrage derivatives valuation beyond payoff diagrams, (d) accounting/financial reporting skill that supports fundamental risk sensing (quality of earnings, off-balance-sheet exposures, pensions/stock comp), and (e) institutional-style portfolio construction (constraints, liquidity, risk budgets, rebalancing, execution-cost measurement and market microstructure). These emphases are explicitly reflected in Level III ÔÇťcommon coreÔÇŁ coverage (asset allocation, portfolio construction, performance measurement, derivatives & risk management) and in the specialized pathway design. ţłÇciteţłéturn3view0ţłéturn7view0ţłéturn8view0ţłü

## Executive summary

The FRM (Financial Risk Manager) curriculum is designed around a core shift that many experienced retail traders have **not** had to make: from *ÔÇťmake good trades and manage my own P&LÔÇŁ* to *ÔÇťdesign, validate, govern, and communicate risk frameworks for an institution under constraints (capital, liquidity, regulation, model risk, operational resilience).ÔÇŁ* ţłÇciteţłéturn33view0ţłéturn16search2ţłü

Using the most recent **official, publicly retrievable** GARP material (the **FRM Candidate Guide 2025**, which includes **topics, weights, and ÔÇťbroad knowledge pointsÔÇŁ subtopics**) and cross-checking with **official GARP 2026 pages** (showing that 2026 Learning Objectives/Study Guide exist but require a gated download form), this report maps the full Part I + Part II topic structure into a **trader-tailored knowledge map** and a **gap matrix**. ţłÇciteţłéturn33view0ţłéturn10search1ţłéturn10search7ţłéturn16search0ţłü

The biggest ÔÇťprofessional risk managerÔÇŁ deltas for a typical experienced retail trader (style/instruments unspecified) cluster into five areas:

1. **Statistical inference & model discipline (Quantitative Analysis)**: hypothesis testing, regression diagnostics, time-series modeling, simulation methodology, and especially **machine learning as a risk tool rather than a trading edge**. ţłÇciteţłéturn34view0ţłü  
2. **Risk models as governance artifacts (Valuation & Risk Models + Market Risk)**: VaR/ES are treated as **model+process+controls** (mapping, backtesting, stress design, limitations) rather than just a number. ţłÇciteţłéturn35view4ţłéturn37search3ţłéturn36search3ţłü  
3. **Credit risk and structured finance**: PD/LGD/EL/UL logic, credit VaR, counterparty exposure profiles, and structured products are often absent from retail experience unless you traded credit or OTC derivatives professionally. ţłÇciteţłéturn34view4ţłü  
4. **Liquidity & treasury risk**: liquidity metrics, cash-flow modeling and liquidity stress testing, funding models, FTP, cross-currency funding, and balance-sheet constraintsÔÇöhigh relevance if you want to understand how ÔÇťreal moneyÔÇŁ and banks manage risk. ţłÇciteţłéturn35view0ţłü  
5. **Operational risk & resilience**: cyber/third-party outsourcing/model risk validation, Basel framing, and stress testing banksÔÇötypically low in retail trading but central to institutional risk roles. ţłÇciteţłéturn34view4ţłéturn37search2ţłéturn37search1ţłü  

If your goal is to identify gaps vs. a professional risk manager, your highest-ROI path is:

**(i)** lock down Quant + Market/Valuation toolchains Ôćĺ **(ii)** add institutional ÔÇťconstraintsÔÇŁ layers (liquidity, credit, operational resilience, Basel) Ôćĺ **(iii)** practice integrating outputs into *decisions, limits, escalation, and reporting*.
