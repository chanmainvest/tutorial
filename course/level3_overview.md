## Part 1: Reading Section

### Level Overview

Level 3 introduces two of the most powerful and widely misunderstood areas of finance: options and fixed income. This is not about speculation -- it is about adding precision tools to your investment toolkit. Options for income generation and hedging, fixed income for stability and cash flow.

### What You Will Learn

**Weeks 25-30: Options**
- Introduction to options -- calls, puts, and how they really work
- Options as enhanced order types, not gambling instruments
- Covered calls for generating income from stocks you own
- Cash-secured puts for getting paid to buy stocks at target prices
- The Greeks -- delta, theta, vega -- the forces that move option prices
- Credit spreads and iron condors for defined-risk income

**Weeks 31-36: Fixed Income**
- Yield curves and what their shape tells us about the economy
- Duration and convexity -- measuring interest rate risk
- Credit analysis and bond selection
- How rate changes ripple across all asset classes
- Advanced financial statement analysis -- detecting earnings quality
- Building an income-generating portfolio combining dividends, bonds, and options

### What You Will Be Able to Do After Level 3

- Write covered calls and cash-secured puts to generate consistent income
- Construct defined-risk option strategies like credit spreads
- Read and interpret yield curves for economic signals
- Evaluate corporate credit quality and select appropriate bonds
- Detect accounting red flags in financial statements
- Build a portfolio designed to produce steady cash flow
